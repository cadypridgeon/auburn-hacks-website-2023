AUBURN HACKS

2/11/23-2/12/23

ABOUT

Auburn Hacks is a free, 24-hour, annual hackathon event held in the spring at Auburn University. During this weekend-long event, hundreds of students collaborate to create technological projects backed by innovation and passion.

At Auburn Hacks, participants come together in teams of four to create technical projects over the course of one day. At the end of the hackathon, teams enter their projects to win cash prizes. If you don't have much coding experience, don't worry! We have prize categories for beginners too.

We will provide free workshops, activities, swag, food, and more for everyone who participates! Plus,Auburn Hacks is the largest hackathon in Alabama - you don't want to miss out!


Join us at Auburn Hacks from February 11th-12th, 2023!

FAQ

GENERAL

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event, typically spanning a day or two, where people come together to collaborate and create a project of their choosing. Hackathons are usually coding events, but programming experience is not required!

When and where is Auburn Hacks?

Auburn Hacks will start Saturday, February 11, 2023 and end Sunday, February 12, 2023.
It is located in the Brown-Kopel Engineering Student Achievement Center on Auburn University's campus.

What goes on at Auburn Hacks?

At Auburn Hacks, hundreds of people come together to learn more about coding and create projects of their choosing. Outside of working on their projects, participants can attend workshops, activities, and tech talks to improve their programming skills.

What is the theme?

This year's theme for Auburn Hacks is tropical!

Why is there a theme?

Auburn Hacks uses the theme to elevate the hackathon experience by providing participants with a cohesive atmosphere for workshops and activities. Additionally, participants can use the theme as inspiration for their projects.

Are there any rules?

Our biggest rule is that all work must be done at Auburn Hacks. Also, everyone who participates must follow the MLH Code of Conduct.

What are the COVID-19 regulations?

Auburn Hacks will be held in accordance with university policies. Currently, the hackathon will be in-person, and masks will not be required. However, if you are exposed to COVID-19, please follow the CDC guidelines to determine whether you should participate.

Have more questions?

If you have any other questions, please email us here!

PARTICIPANTS

Who can participate?

Anyone who is currently a college student or graduated in the past year can participate. This includes undergraduate and graduate students.
Auburn Hacks welcomes participants from any university!

How do I register?

Please register here! Everyone who wants to secure a spot at the hackthon must register.
If you have any questions about registration, email us here.

When does registration close?

Registration closes February 10, 2023 at 11:59 PM CST.

Are walk-ins accepted?

We accept walk-ins if we have available spots, but it is not guaranteed. To secure a spot, please register here.

How do teams work?

Each team consists of four participants. If you would like to create a team before the event, please feel free! However, if you do not have a team by the hackathon, don’t worry! We will team creation available on the day of the event.

What should I bring?

Laptop, chargers, and any other technology you might need.
Since the event runs 24 hours, you may also want to bring a sleeping bag/pillow/blanket, deodorant, toothbrush/toothpaste, and contacts/glasses.
Most importantly, don’t forget to bring enthusiasm and a positive attitude!

What should I wear?

Wear anything that you will be comfortable in for 24 hours. If you want to join in on the theme, feel free to wear tropical attire (hawaiian shirt, floral patterns, etc.)!

Can I volunteer instead of participate?

Yes! Register here to volunteer during Auburn Hacks. Volunteering is a great way to see what all goes on at a hackathon behind the scenes.

LOGISTICS

How much does it cost to participate?

Nothing! It is completely free to participate in Auburn Hacks!

Is the hackathon in-person?

Yes, Auburn Hacks is scheduled to be an entirely in-person event.

Are there going to be prizes?

Yes, there will be cash prizes for the top three intermediate projects. In addition to these, there will be a beginner project prize and sponsor prizes.*
* This information is subject to change.

Will there be food?

Yes, participants will receive free lunch, dinner, breakfast, and snacks.

Can I sleep at Auburn Hacks?

Yes, we will have a room dedicated to sleeping in the evening hours. If you plan on sleeping, please bring your own sleeping bag/pillows/blankets.

Where is the schedule?

As the hackathon nears, a live site will be published with the full hackathon schedule and other resources. If you register, you will receive an email with the live site and the full schedule attached.

How do I get to Auburn Hacks?

The entirety of Auburn Hacks will take place in the Brown-Kopel Engineering Student Achievement Center on Auburn University's campus. Please park in the “A” parking lots near the 320 West Mag apartment complex or Lowder.

Will there be travel compensation?

Unfortunately, Auburn Hacks will not provide compensation to those traveling to Auburn. Please email us here if you have any questions.

TECH

Can I make anything I want?

Yes! All appropriate software projects are welcome. Unfortunately, Auburn Hacks does not support hardware projects at this time.

Can I bring code that I've previously programmed?

No, all code must be written during the hackathon.

Can I start working on my project before the event?

No, all code must be written during the hackathon.

Should I attend if I have no coding experience?

Yes! We have workshops for beginner and intermediate participants. We also have a prize category for beginner teams. If you want to get started coding, Auburn Hacks would be a great event for you!

Is there a prize category for beginners?

Yes! To level the playing field for beginner programmers, we have decided to add a beginner project prize category this year.
We determine beginner eligibility by reviewing participants’ registration information.

What are some resources I can look at before the hackathon?

We are working on creating a resource pack for beginners to prepare for Auburn Hacks. If you register, we will notify you when it's complete!
